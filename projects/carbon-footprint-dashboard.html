Sustainability Data Visualisation UI Design

Degroof Petercam

Turning dense carbon emissions data into clear, actionable insight — a sustainability metrics UI designed for non-technical users at a green-tech startup.

01 — Problem

Data that only data scientists could read.

The startup had built a powerful carbon accounting engine — it could ingest emissions data across Scope 1, 2, and 3, model reduction scenarios, and benchmark against industry standards. But the interface had been designed by engineers for engineers. When they started selling to sustainability managers and finance teams, the product fell flat.

The dashboard showed everything at once: thirty-seven data points on a single screen, six different chart types, and a colour system that conveyed no consistent meaning.

"We know the data is right. The problem is our users open the dashboard and immediately feel overwhelmed. They close it and email us instead." — Product Manager

02 — Approach

One number to rule them all.

Research surfaced two distinct user types: Sustainability Managers who needed a clear narrative, and Finance Directors who needed specificity. We structured the IA around three questions users actually asked: "How are we doing overall?" → "Where are our biggest emissions?" → "What can we do about it?"

We reduced from six chart types to three. Colour was given systematic meaning: green for on-track, amber for at-risk. We also applied green design principles to the interface itself — minimising JavaScript, using lightweight chart libraries, and benchmarking the page's own carbon footprint.

Minimised JS dependencies, used lightweight chart libraries, and benchmarked the page's carbon footprint before and after. Redesigned version was 68% lighter.

04 — Outcomes

Clear data. Engaged users. Fewer support calls.

Three months after launch, weekly active usage of dashboard features had nearly quadrupled. Users were spending more time exploring the reduction opportunities — the behaviour the product team had always wanted but never achieved.
