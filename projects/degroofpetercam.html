01 — Problem

A 40% drop-off nobody had investigated.

The existing onboarding flow had a persistent 40% drop-off on the third step. Analytics showed the problem clearly; they didn't show why. When we reviewed support tickets, a pattern emerged — a significant proportion came from users with assistive technology who couldn't complete the flow at all.

The form had no visible focus states. Error messages weren't linked to their inputs. The multi-step progress indicator was decoration, not information. Keyboard users could tab into a modal but couldn't escape it without a mouse.

"I've tried three times. The form submits but nothing happens and there's no feedback from my screen reader." — User feedback ticket

02 — Approach

Fix the foundation. Improve the whole.

We ran a structured audit using axe DevTools, manual keyboard testing, and screen reader walkthroughs with VoiceOver and NVDA. Failures clustered into four areas: focus management, form labelling, colour contrast, and status feedback.

Accessibility fixes rarely stay isolated — when you fix a focus state, you improve clarity for all users. The redesign addressed every audit failure and used those fixes as an opportunity to improve the overall UX of the flow. The progress indicator became a proper ARIA role, every form field got a persistent visible label, and error messages were rewritten to be specific and actionable.

04 — Outcomes

Better access. Better completion. Better product.

Post-launch metrics showed a 23% improvement in onboarding completion rate overall — not just for users with assistive technology. Support tickets from users encountering errors in the flow dropped by 61% within the first month. The product passed a third-party WCAG 2.1 AA audit with no critical or serious failures.
